Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus

What is Spina bifida?

Spina bifida is a neural tube defect that occurs during fetal development when the spinal column does not close properly, resulting in varying degrees of damage to the spinal cord and nerves. This condition can lead to physical and neurological impairments, including paralysis, bladder and bowel dysfunction and cognitive challenges.

What is Hydrocephalus?

Hydrocephalus is a condition characterized by an excessive accumulation of cerebrospinal fluid within the brain, leading to increased pressure and swelling. This can result in a range of symptoms including headaches, nausea, vision problems and cognitive difficulties. Hydrocephalus often coexists with Spina bifida but can also occur independently.

How common are Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus?

Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus are relatively rare conditions, occurring in approximately 1 in every 1,000 live births worldwide. However, the prevalence may vary depending on factors such as geographic location, genetic predisposition and access to prenatal care.

What causes Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus?

The exact causes of Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus are not fully understood, but they are believed to result from a combination of genetic, environmental and nutritional factors. Certain risk factors such as maternal folic acid deficiency, exposure to certain medications or toxins during pregnancy and genetic predisposition, may increase the likelihood of these conditions.

Can Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus be prevented?

While it may not be possible to prevent Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus entirely, certain preventive measures can reduce the risk. Ensuring adequate intake of folic acid before and during pregnancy, avoiding exposure to harmful substances and receiving early prenatal care are important steps in minimizing the risk of these conditions.

What are the treatment options for Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus?

Treatment for Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus depends on the severity of the condition and associated complications. Surgical interventions, such as closure of the spinal defect and placement of a shunt to drain excess cerebrospinal fluid, are common treatment modalities. Additionally, supportive therapies, including physical therapy, occupational therapy and educational support, may be beneficial in managing symptoms and improving quality of life.

Is there a cure for Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus?

Currently, there is no cure for Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus but various treatment options are available to manage symptoms and improve quality of life. These include surgical interventions, supportive therapies and ongoing medical management to address associated complications and optimize outcomes.

How can I access medical care for Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus in Kenya?

Child-Help Kenya works closely with healthcare providers and medical facilities across Kenya to ensure individuals with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus have access to specialized medical care and treatment options. You can contact Child-Help Kenya directly for assistance in locating a healthcare provider or clinic near you that specializes in the management of these conditions.

Can Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus be diagnosed before birth?

Prenatal screening and diagnostic tests such as ultrasound imaging and amniocentesis, can sometimes detect signs of Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus during pregnancy. However, a definitive diagnosis may require further evaluation and testing by healthcare professionals specializing in fetal medicine.

What support services are available for adults living with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus?

Child-Help Kenya works with partners to provide a range of support services tailored to the unique needs of adults living with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus. These may include vocational training, job placement assistance, independent living skills development, access to healthcare and rehabilitation services and peer support networks to promote social integration and empowerment.

Child-Help Kenya

How does Child-Help Kenya help individuals affected by Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus?

Child-Help Kenya empowers organizations that provide a range of support services and resources for individuals and families affected by Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus in Kenya. These include access to medical care, educational support, advocacy resources and community outreach programs aimed at raising awareness and promoting inclusion for individuals with these conditions.

What support services does Child-Help Kenya offer?

Through it's implementing partners, Child-Help Kenya offers a range of support services tailored to the unique needs of individuals and families affected by Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus. These include counseling and psychosocial support, medical consultations, access to specialized clinics and treatment centers, educational resources and community-based initiatives to promote inclusion and empowerment.

How can I get involved with Child-Help Kenya?

There are several ways to get involved with us and support our mission of improving the lives of individuals with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus in Kenya. These includes volunteering your time and skills, participating in fundraising events and campaigns, donating financial resources or in-kind contributions, social media support, advocating for policy change and awareness-raising initiatives and spreading the word about our work within your community and networks.

What educational resources are available for individuals with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus?

Child-Help Kenya provides educational resources and support services to help individuals with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus access quality education and achieve their full potential. These include educational materials, workshops, annual symposia and advocacy efforts aimed at promoting inclusive education policies, providing academic support and addressing barriers to learning faced by individuals with these conditions.

How can I request assistance or support from Child-Help Kenya?

If you or a loved one is affected by Spina bifida or Hydrocephalus and requires assistance or support, you can reach out to Child-Help Kenya or visit our Headquaters in Nairobi. A member of our team will be available to listen to your concerns, provide information and guide you on available support services and resources.

Does Child-Help Kenya offer financial assistance for medical treatment?

Child-Help Kenya does not offer financial assistance directly to help individuals with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus but empowers healthcare facilities and to promote access to essential medical treatment and services. This support encompasses charity-case surgical procedures, medical consultations, diagnostic tests and assistive devices. Eligibility criteria and application processes may vary, so it's advisable to contact us directly for more information.

Are there support groups or networks for individuals and families affected by Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus?

Child-Help Kenya works closely with Spina bifida & Hydrocephalus Association of Kenya (SHAK) to facilitate support groups and networks accross Kenya, for individuals and families affected by Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus to connect with others facing similar challenges, share experiences and access peer support. SHAK coordinates these support groups meetings and provides opportunities for socialization, education as well as personal and emotional support.

How does Child-Help Kenya raise awareness about Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus?

Child-Help Kenya conducts the Give-Life Charity Run / Awareness Walk every September of the year, being the global Hydrocephalus awareness month. Every October, Child-Help Kenya joins the world to Mark the World Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus Day through the Annual SBH National Symposium which incorporates the SBH Champions Award to acknowledge the efforts of Child-Help Kenya's partners towards individuals with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus. Every December, Child-Help Kenya runs SBH Music Concerts, a benefit program that brings together musicians, performers and attendees to support our causes through ticket sales, donations and sponsorships. Child-Help Kenya also partners with the private sector and corporate entities to raise funds through the 5 Cents For Life Campaign. These events are geared towards raising awareness about Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus within communities, among healthcare professionals, policymakers, other stakeholders and the general public.

Can I make a donation to Child-Help Kenya?

Yes of course, Child-Help Kenya relies on the generosity of donors and supporters to fund their programs and initiatives. You can make a donation to Child-Help Kenya through various channels, including online donations, bank transfers, fundraising events and in-kind contributions. Your contribution helps to support our mission of improving the lives of individuals with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus in Kenya.
