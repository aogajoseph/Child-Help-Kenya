Who We Are

Child-Help Kenya is a duly registered nonprofit organization, affiliated to Child-Help International with and mandated to support individuals and organizations serving or caring for people with the Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus in Kenya.

The Child-Help Kenya consortium creates a supportive environment that unites all stakeholders involved in ensuring the well-being of affected individuals. Our Processes are anchored on the following areas of focus: High Profile Advocacy & Awareness on Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus, Resource Mobilization for our initiatives and Monitoring and Evaluation of service delivery by our implementing partners.

This unity of purpose encourages strategic partnerships and prevents duplication of efforts. Thereby, maximizing the efficacy and efficiency of service delivery for the people we serve.

What We Do

Mobilizing Resources

We partner with individuals and organizations to mobilize financial and non-financial resources to fund initiatives geared towards the promoting the well-being of individuals affected by Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus.

Learn More

Supporting Individuals

We collaborate with teens, youth and adults with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus in skill development and talent nurturing and offer access to resources, equipment, other necessities and available opportunities.

Learn More

Supporting Organizations

Our strategic partnerships with various organizations ensures efficiency in the provision of specialized care and service delivery tailored to the individual needs of people with Spina bifida and Hydrocephalus.

Learn More

Monitoring & Evaluation

We have developed tools for monitoring and evaluating the works done by our partners in the implementation of our initiatives. This helps us assess success, measure impact and extend our reach to wider regions in the community.

Our Mandate:

Registered as a nonprofit organization and working in affiliation with Child-Help International our mandate is to foster strategic partnerships to maximize the efficacy and efficiency of service delivery by key partners and prevent duplication of efforts.

We achieve this by focusing on the following key areas:

  1. High Profile Advocacy and Awareness.
  2. Resource Mobilization.
  3. Monitoring and Evaluation.
